Opportunity Rover

2003 - 2019

Mission Timline:

  • June 10, 2003: Spirit and Opportunity launched a month apart.
  • July 8, 2003: Opportunity launched, managed by NASA's Launch Services Program.
  • January 25, 2004: Opportunity landed on Mars in the Eagle crater.
  • June - December 2004: Explored the Endurance crater.
  • Late 2004: Discovered Heat Shield Rock, an intact meteorite.
  • October 2005 - March 2006: Investigated Erebus crater.
  • September 2006: Reached Victoria crater.
  • June 2007: Returned to Duck Bay at Victoria crater.
  • September 2007: Entered Victoria crater for detailed study.
  • August 2008: Departed Victoria crater for Endeavour crater.
  • August 9, 2011: Arrived at Endeavour crater.
  • 2011 - 2013: Analyzed Cape York, investigating phyllosilicates.
  • May 2013: Headed towards Solander Point.
  • December 2014: Opportunity suffers from "amnesia" evets, fails to write to non-volatile memory.
  • May 23, 2015: Rover configured to operate in RAM-only mode, avoiding use of non-volatile memory.
  • June 10, 2018: Last communication from Opportunity.
  • February 12, 2019: Final commands transmitted to Opportunity.
  • February 13, 2019: Official end of the Opportunity mission declared by NASA.
  • June 10, 2018 (sol 5111): Last communication from the rover indicated high atmospheric opacity and reduced energy production.
